# IDC Perdita

A block Deseret Alphabet font based on the hand-drawn â€œOversize Specimenâ€, ca. 1856, CHL MS 16662, img. 3/4. ## Characters

The specimen includes thirteen vowels, one more than usual; ğœ is omitted, probably inadvertently. Between the glyphs for the FATE and PALM vowels (assuming standard collation) appears what is most likely a glyph for the SQUARE vowel, a combination of ğ‡ and ğˆ (cf. Glyph shapes for the consonant in RUE and the vowel in STRUT suggest this object is from the era of the Woodruff specimen (1856) or the St.\ Louis typeface (1857). Includes minuscule and majuscule glyphs that differ primarily in size. Most majuscules have serifs whereas minuscules do not. Glyphs in some cases resemble a Gothic Book hand. ## Production

```sh
mpost src/perdita.mp
```

The majuscules are produced in FontForge from the SVGs at the settings (in `src/perdita.mp`):

```
font_size  := 72pt;
pen_height := 0.12 * font_size;
```

- Regular corresponds to 0.12

The following adjustments are made manually:

1. All are scaled 80% and moved `(-65.6, 90.5)`.
2. SVGs are overlapped and in most cases simplified once.
4. Kerning is manually set at +100 to +150.

The minuscules are copied and produced at the settings:

1. All are scaled 80% and moved `(-53.35, -80)`.

Finally, `Metrics`â†’`Auto Width` is applied to adjust general kerning margins at `150`.
